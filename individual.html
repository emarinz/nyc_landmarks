Individual NYC Landmarks

From a wooden one-story farmhouse, to soaring skyscrapers, like the Empire State Building and the Chrysler Building, individual landmarks come in all shapes and sizes.

Individual landmarks are not limited to large structures such as buildings and bridges. One of the most important and essential parts of New York City’s historical fabric is its “street furniture”—lamp posts, street clocks and sign posts that enhance and maintain the intimacy and scale of neighborhood blocks. Along with the interior and scenic landmark types, all individual structures must be at least 30 years old to become a landmark.

FUN FACT: The Carroll Street Bridge in Brooklyn is one of the few remaining retractile bridges in the United States. For more information on this bridge, go to forgotten-ny.com.
