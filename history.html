History of NYC Landmarks

The New York City Landmarks Preservation Commission is responsible for protecting New York City’s architecturally, historically, and culturally significant buildings and sites.

The Commission was created in 1965 through groundbreaking legislation signed by Mayor Robert F. Wagner in response to the mounting losses of historically significant buildings in New York City, most infamously the old Pennsylvania Station. One of the most prominent decisions in which the Commission was involved was the preservation of the Grand Central Terminal with the assistance of Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis. To learn more about the fight to save Grand Central that ended up in the U.S. Supreme Court, go to wnyc.org.

FUN FACT: The Main Concourse in Grand Central has an elaborately decorated astronomical ceiling. For more information on the ceiling and the error it contains, go to untappedcities.com.
