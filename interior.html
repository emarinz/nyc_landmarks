Interior NYC Landmarks

The very first interior landmark named is the main branch
of the New York Public Library on Fifth Ave at 42nd Street. The interior of this great building is as magnificent as its exterior.

Interior landmarks are buildings that are customarily open or accessible to the public or
to which the public is customarily invited, such as
a theater, a courthouse, or office building. They
must also meet the criteria for individual landmarks. Many landmarks are both individual and interior landmarks. The more famous examples include the Chrysler Building, the Empire State Building, and Grand Central Terminal.

FUN FACT: There are 12 subway stations that were designated
as interior landmarks in 1979. To watch a video on the City Hall station, which is no longer in use, go to youtube.com.
