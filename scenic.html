Scenic NYC Landmarks

There may only be ten scenic landmarks, but they cover a lot of ground. Central Park, the largest of the scenic landmarks at 843 acres, is equal to 6% of Manhattan’s total acreage.

Scenic landmarks are city-owned parks and other landscape features. The majority of the scenic landmarks are located in Manhattan: Central Park, Fort Tryon Park, Riverside Park and Riverside Drive, Morningside Park, Bryant Park, Grand Army Plaza and Verdi Square. The balance are all located in Brooklyn: Prospect Park, Eastern Parkway and Ocean Parkway.

FUN FACT: Five scenic landmarks, from parks to parkways,
were designed by Frederick Law Olmsted. For more information
on Olmsted’s NYC parks, go to nycgovparks.org.
