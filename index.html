New York City Landmarks

From the Statue of Liberty,
to a roller coaster ride
and a tree in Brooklyn,
New York City landmarks are
as diverse as the people
who inhabit the city.

The New York City Landmarks Preservation Commission was formed in 1965. There are now over 36,000 landmark properties most of which are located in historic districts across all five boroughs
of the city. The total number of protected sites also includes 1,398 individual landmarks, 119 interior landmarks, and 10 scenic landmarks. Some of these sites are also National Historic Landmarks, and many are on the National Register of Historic Places.

FUN FACT: The Magnolia Grandiflora tree is the only living landmark in New York City. For more information on this landmark and the woman who saved it, go to brownstoner.com.
